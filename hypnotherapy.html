What is hypnotherapy?

Hypnotherapy is a form of complementary therapy that utilises the power of positive suggestion to bring about subconscious change to our thoughts, feelings and behaviour.

The therapeutic process aims to alter our state of consciousness in a way that relaxes the conscious part of the mind whilst simultaneously stimulating and focusing the subconscious part. This heightened state of awareness allows the therapist to then make positive, often life-changing suggestions.

Hypnotherapy is a recognised therapy that can help with many physical and emotinal issues, including:

  • Stopping smoking
  • Weight management
  • Stress and anxiety
  • IBS
  • Phobias
  • Addiction and habit disorders
  • Confidence and self esteem
  • Obsessive compulsive disorders
  • Sexual problems

In recent years, hypnotherapy has gained increasing recognistion in the world of medicine after a recommendation from the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) recognised hypnotherapy as a treatment for Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS).

Many people use hypnotherapy for weight loss and smoking cessation. Hypnosis for weight loss, for example, is proven to be more effective than dieting because it aims to alter a person’s perception of food and eating rather than simply limiting calorie intake for a temporary period of time. It is also proven to be three times more effective when giving up smoking than the use of nicotine patches.
